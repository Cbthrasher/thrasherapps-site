Answer key · trainer copy
Show answer key
  1. B — One position vs. all positions changes the entire troubleshooting path.
  2. B — A clogged filter is the most common single-position cause.
  3. B — STP/motor deactivated and shear valve closed before opening a line.
  4. B — Repeated trips mean a short or ground fault; stop and investigate, don't keep resetting.
  5. B — Steady green = normal operation (blinking green = communicating/dispensing).
  6. C — White cap = regular unleaded.
  7. True.
  8. False — Never open a dispenser while a customer is dispensing.
  9. True — The Wayne CPU board is the equivalent of Gilbarco's CRIND.
  10. False — Over-tightening cracks the cap or cuts the o-ring and causes a leak; hand-tight plus a small snug.
  11. Upstream / line- or pump-side. Any two: STP not running/cycling; line leak detector tripped; check valve at the STP; actual line leak; power to the STP; tank empty or wrong product mapping.
  12. Regular = white; premium = red; diesel = yellow or green.
  13. Stop the source if safe (close the shear valve / shut down the STP), contain the spill, notify per procedure, and document what you found and did.
  14. Stop and call your manager — do not pour it.
