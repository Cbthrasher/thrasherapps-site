Answer key · trainer copy
Show answer key
  1. B — Check for vapor/odor and keep ignition sources away before opening.
  2. B — Aluminum or other non-magnetic, fuel-compatible metal — never PVC.
  3. B — Fuel in a sump is a safety finding; something is leaking. Document and escalate.
  4. B — Stage II captures vapors at the nozzle during dispensing and returns them to the tank (Stage I is during delivery, back to the truck).
  5. B — Stop when you start pulling fuel instead of water.
  6. B — A MagVFC controls variable-speed FE Petro STPs (fixed-speed uses a contactor + capacitor).
  7. False — Pump it out and dispose properly; even "just water" may carry a fuel sheen.
  8. True — Usually about 90–95% capacity.
  9. False — Wait one full minute after the LED display goes blank; the capacitor bank stores lethal voltage.
  10. True — Unsealed conduit is the #1 water path into sumps.
  11. Any three: delivery (water in the supply); leaking spill bucket / damaged fill cap; condensation; containment failure (compromised fittings/seals).
  12. Any three: ATG high water alarm; ATG water level reading; manual stick with water-finding paste; customer complaints.
  13. The fill adapter must come out first; note the drop tube's position/orientation so it goes back the same way.
  14. A containment basin around the fill pipe that catches fuel drips when the delivery hose is disconnected, keeping product out of the ground.
