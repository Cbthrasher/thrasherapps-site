Daily training routine (use every day)

Office block (30–45 minutes)

  1. 5 min: recap yesterday (what we saw, what we learned)
  2. 15–20 min: micro-lesson (today’s topic)
  3. 5–10 min: quick drill (ATG “find it fast” or part ID)
  4. 5 min: set 2–3 field objectives (must be performed today)

Field block (real calls)

On every call, assign a trainee role:

  • Customer Lead: greet, set expectations, updates, closeout
  • ATG Operator: TLS work + evidence capture
  • Site Mapper: confirm locations + photos + notes
  • Hands-On Tech: sump/spill bucket/tank work + repairs

Rule: No customer goes >60 minutes without an update.

End-of-day signoff (5 minutes)
  • Trainer initials the day’s checklist items
  • Note what must be repeated tomorrow
  • Save evidence: photos + notes + console screenshots (as applicable)

Safety (must follow every job)

Pad / jobsite safety checklist
  • PPE on before stepping onto the work area
  • Control traffic: cones, truck placement, spotter when needed
  • Eliminate ignition sources near open sumps/spill buckets
  • Keep tools organized (no trip hazards, no loose parts left behind)
  • Stop-work authority: if conditions are unsafe, stop and escalate
Electrical safety boundaries (simple rule set)
  • Know what you’re authorized/trained to do vs what requires escalation
  • If uncertain: pause, make safe, call lead
  • Never “wing it” on power/control wiring
  • Verify shutoffs and communicate impact before taking equipment down (unless emergency)
Driving safety basics
  • No rushing, no aggressive driving, no distractions
  • Back up with a spotter when visibility is limited
  • Secure equipment before driving (no loose tools/hoses)
Spill response mindset (quick)
  • Stop the source (if safe)
  • Contain (prevent spread)
  • Notify per company procedure
  • Document what happened and what was done

Customer service & communication (required)

Greeting + expectation script

“Hi, I’m ______ with Petro Plus. I understand we’re here for ______. I’m going to inspect and run checks, then I’ll update you with what I find and the next steps.”

Update script (every 30–60 minutes)

“Quick update: we’ve confirmed ______. Next I’m going to ______. I’ll update you again in about ______ minutes.”

Closeout script

“We found ______. We corrected it by ______. We verified by ______. If you see ______ again, call us and mention ______.”

Never say this

  • “That’s not our problem.”
  • “Someone else messed this up.”
  • “I don’t know.” (Replace with “I’m going to verify with my lead.”)
  • “You have a leak.” (Unless confirmed by procedure and authorized to state it)

Rule: Be calm. Be clear. No blame language. Leave the site cleaner than you found it.

Water removal (spill buckets / sumps / tanks)

This is a core service line. Be consistent, document everything, and avoid contamination mistakes.

Spill bucket water removal – standard workflow
  1. Confirm bucket ID/location (match site map)
  2. Inspect condition (before photos)
  3. Remove water using approved method/equipment
  4. Check for likely intrusion causes (seal, lid, drain issues, obvious damage)
  5. After photos + notes + recommendation (if needed)
Sump water removal – standard workflow
  1. Make safe (traffic control, ignition awareness, lighting)
  2. Open and inspect (before photos)
  3. Remove water using approved method/equipment
  4. Identify likely intrusion path (boots, covers, conduit, fittings, cracks)
  5. Verify alarm/sensor condition (if applicable) + document
  6. After photos + closeout to customer
Tank water work – notes
  • Follow Petro Plus procedures and escalation rules
  • Document: what triggered the call, what was verified, what was removed, and what was recommended
  • When unsure: stop and escalate (do not guess)

Equipment basics (what to recognize first)

Drops / fill / vapor (core familiarity)
  • Know the common parts we service and what “normal” looks like
  • Common failures: water intrusion points, damaged seals, loose fittings, broken lids/covers, compromised boots
  • Rule: verify before replacing parts—document evidence
Sumps (tank vs dispenser)
  • Identify which sump type you’re in and what components are present
  • Look for intrusion paths and conditions that cause repeat water calls
  • Before/after photos always
Electrical support equipment
  • Mag VFC: recognize + know typical symptoms when failing
  • Red Jacket relays / IsoTrol: recognize + know what they protect/control at a high level
  • E-stops: location awareness + safe communication before use
  • Boundary rule: if it’s outside your authorization/training—pause and escalate
Dispensers (Gilbarco / Wayne) – universal symptom model
  • Dead / no power
  • Won’t authorize
  • Slow / no flow
  • Leak / alarm
