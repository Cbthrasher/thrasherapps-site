Safety

Non-negotiables for pad work, electrical boundaries, driving, and staying aware on site.

Pad / jobsite essentials

Before you start
  • PPE on before stepping into the work area
  • Control traffic (cones/truck position/spotter when needed)
  • Ignition awareness near open sumps/spill buckets
  • Keep tools/parts controlled (no trip hazards, no loose hardware)
Traffic control & visibility
  • Use your truck to block / shield the work area from traffic
  • Set out cones around the work zone
  • Wear a hi-vis vest or bright colors so you're easy to see
  • Use a spotter whenever sightlines are poor
PPE & fire
  • Wear safety glasses for the work
  • Keep an up-to-date fire extinguisher within reach of the work area
  • Check the extinguisher gauge / tag before you rely on it
Stop-work rule

If conditions are unsafe or unclear, stop, make safe, and escalate. No guessing.

Electrical boundaries

Simple rule set
  • Only do what you’re trained/authorized to do
  • If uncertain: pause, make safe, call lead
  • Never “wing it” on power/control wiring
  • Communicate impact before shutdown (unless emergency)
Verify it's dead — every time
  • Always verify the voltage is off with a meter before you touch a conductor — don't trust a label or a flipped switch alone
  • Lock out / tag out the breaker any time you turn it off or walk away from it, so no one re-energizes it while you're on the wires
  • Prove your meter works: test it on a known live source, then the dead circuit, then the live source again (live–dead–live)
Megohmmeter (megger) testing
  • Double-check you're on the right wires before you apply test voltage
  • Cap off the end that isn't clamped by the meter so the energized lead can't contact anything
  • Make sure the wire is not still connected to motors or sensors — megging into them can damage the equipment
  • Confirm the conductor is fully isolated before testing
  • Free of gas fumes — never megger where fumes may be present
Fumes + electricity
Never check live voltage when fumes are present. If you smell fuel or vapor, stop, make the area safe / ventilate, and take no live electrical reading until it's clear.

Driving safety

Basics
  • No texting and driving — phone down, eyes on the road
  • Set up your GPS before you put the vehicle in drive, never while moving
  • No rushing, no distractions
  • Back up with a spotter when visibility is limited
  • Secure equipment before driving (no loose hoses/tools)
Rule: Anything that needs your hands or eyes — GPS, phone, paperwork — happens while parked, not while moving.

Spill mindset (quick)

What to do
  • Stop the source (if safe)
  • Contain and prevent spread
  • Notify per company procedure
  • Document what happened + what you did

Situational awareness & personal safety

Stay aware on site
  • Always be aware of your surroundings — use your senses: sight, sound, smell, and touch
  • No earphones or loud music — you need to hear pumps, traffic, and people
  • Know where your exits are and keep a clear path out
Crime or violence
If you feel unsafe because of crime or violence: make your work area safe when applicable, leave, and call your manager. Your safety comes before the job — no equipment is worth it.
